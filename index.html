How UK Pharmacy Works

The key to profitability is buying medicines at the lowest price and dispensing efficiently. We help you master the purchasing part.

1

Prescriptions

Patients receive prescriptions from doctors and take them to pharmacies. Reimbursement is based on the Drug Tariff.

2

Reimbursement

Pharmacies are reimbursed by the government based on the Drug Tariff, which is updated quarterly.

3

Profitability

Profit is the difference between the purchase price and the reimbursement price. We help you widen that gap.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the prescription payment system work?
Patients pay £9.35 per item at the pharmacy. Pharmacies are then reimbursed by the government based on the Drug Tariff price for each item dispensed.
Why can’t medicines be sold directly on platforms like Amazon?
UK law restricts the sale of medicines to licensed businesses to ensure patient safety and regulatory compliance. This maintains a high standard of care and oversight.
What are the main types of medicines?
  • POM: Prescription Only Medicine (most common)
  • P: Pharmacy Line (over-the-counter, but only from a pharmacy)
  • GSL: General Sales List (can be sold in general retail)
How do you help with supplier management?
We help pharmacies manage relationships with major and niche suppliers, providing data to optimize order quantities, negotiate better terms, and streamline delivery schedules.
